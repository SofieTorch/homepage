I started actively participating in tech communities when I was 15 years old. It started with taking free courses on the weekends, and end up with me giving talks, organizing, and leading communities.

For me, it was just having fun with friends that like the same stuff as me, but after moving to Zürich, I noticed that communities started to get a reputation of "a place to make networking" and eventually get something out of it, like a job.

I think that, as a community yes, part of our mission is to help other people to grow in their careers, but after all these years I'd argue that I got something even better: a place to _be_.

At 16 I met one of my best friends to date (already 8 years!), Fer, because both of us were always, by coincidence, in the same events. We started talking outside of the meetups, give talks together, and eventually even co-founding our own community together with another friends. 

I also met Griss, who was my teacher in one of my first free-courses of "Intro to web development". I asked her to be my mentor for a competition, and we are still friends and keep doing stuff together after moving in the same year (different countries, but we visit each other from time to time, and even organized an online meetup!)

After moving I was worried about not being able to make friends, as I knew nobody here,
so I started to show up on the places where I have been welcomed before: meet ups and conferences. I've attended PyCon US that year, where I met amazing people, who some of them happened to also attend EuroPython. I sent a message to one of them and then we met there, where he introduced me to someone else who introduced me to someone else and I made a friendship that I will never forget, Raffi, I'm still planning on go to visit her!

And the most important person I ever met, Loris, was a speaker at a local meetup in Zürich,
I was incredibly late to the meetup and had no idea he was presenting, I saw him and was like
"hey, did you enjoy the talks?" and we started dating one month later 🩷.

It might sound a bit like "I went to communities and made friends", and yeah it is true, but that's the point, right? I remember how at one of the lighting talks at EuroPython 25 a CPython Core Developer shared with us his story with his wife, finishing the talk with the phrase "I gave you software, but you gave me my life" (referring to the Python community)
